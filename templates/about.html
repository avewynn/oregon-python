What is the Oregon Trail?

Historical Background

The Oregon Trail is a 2,170 mile route that spans from Missouri to Oregon. Hundreds of thousands of settlers, farmers, and miners transversed this trail.

Objective of the Game

The player's goal is to escort a party from Independence, Missouri to Willamette Valley in Oregon. players purchase food, oxen, clothing, ammunition, and spare parts in order to keep their wagon going. Party members can die from various causes, and it is the player's goal to keep as many of them alive as possible. Players are scored at the end based on a variety of factors, including starting profession and members still alive.

Where are all those Features?

Due to budget cuts and time restrictions, the developer of this version of The Oregon Trail created a very barebones version of the original experience. However, dysentery was not forgotten.

Original Games

Originally released in 1971 as a text-based game, The Oregon Trail Series has brought a number of popular spinoffs. The list includes:

  • The Oregon Trail (1971 video game), text-based strategy game
  • Oregon Trail, a 1981 board game
  • The Oregon Trail (1985 video game), an educational computer game based on the 1971 version
  • The Oregon Trail (2009 video game), the 2009 iOS and DSi version of the 1985 game
  • The Oregon Trail (2011 video game), the 2011 version of the 1971 video game
  • The Oregon Trail (card game), a card game based on the video game
